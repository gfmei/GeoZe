Where the time goes

GeoZe builds a knn patch around every superpoint and runs Sinkhorn attention inside each one. SemGeoZe v2 never attends inside a region: it agglomerates the partition, merging adjacent regions in mutual-best-match pairs when they agree both semantically and geometrically. Every step is a scatter-reduce over one edge list, and the neighbour graph comes from multi-curve voting over eight space-filling curves rather than a KD-tree.

Aggregation only — per-point features in, refined features out. The two GeoZe/OpenScene rows are quoted from Table 6 of the paper and were measured on the authors' hardware, so the ratio is indicative rather than a controlled comparison.

The honest reading: SemGeoZe v2 improves region mean pooling by +0.43 mIoU and is far cheaper than GeoZe's scene pipeline — but +0.43 is a small gain, and on nuScenes the merge is neutral (0.2947 vs 0.2995, reaching parity at th_f 0.98). Speed is the contribution; accuracy is parity-plus-a-little. Per-scene results vary in both directions, which is why the viewer above ships a neutral, a typical and a good scene rather than three favourable ones.

Two things a reader should know

ScanNet's mesh over-segmentation is label-perfect. Assigning each segment its dominant ground-truth label scores mIoU exactly 1.000, because ScanNet's annotators labelled those very segments. Part of the per-point → per-region jump is therefore an annotation artefact rather than a method gain. Running on a VCCS partition instead removes the leak and costs about 3.4 mIoU.

Most of the remaining headroom is not aggregation. Pooling over the ground-truth region — a perfect partition — still only reaches 0.6845 mIoU. So of the gap to the oracle, roughly 21 points are VLM/text misalignment that no aggregation can close and only ~13 are reachable at all.
